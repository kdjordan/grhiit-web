## Program Structure Reference

**Session Architecture:**
1. Opening auxiliary work (warmup/primer)
2. Tabata blocks (the mental crucible)
3. Repeat opening auxiliary work (prove you have more)

**Four Core Movements:**
- 8-Count Bodybuilders
- Jump Squats
- Burpees (no push-up variation)
- Flutter Squats

**Auxiliary Work Formats:**
- Precise intervals: 6×3, 10×5, etc.
- Smoker sets: Continuous flow from movement to movement under sustained load

**Tabata Block Progression:**
| Week | Structure | Total Tabata Time |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| 1-2 | 3 blocks × 2 intervals | 3 min |
| 3-4 | 3 blocks × 4 intervals | 6 min |
| 5-6 | 3 blocks × 6 intervals | 9 min |
| 7-8 | 3 blocks × 8 intervals | 12 min |

**Tabata Protocol:**
- 20 seconds max effort
- 10 seconds recovery
- 2:1 work-to-rest ratio (the "mother of all intervals")

**Why This Progression Works:**
- Week 1: More recovery windows = more mental escape routes
- Week 8: Fewer breaks = learn to sustain intensity when quitting feels like the only option
- The nervous system learns to maintain max output under sustained load
- This rewires your relationship with discomfort

**Key Insight:**
The auxiliary work bookending the Tabata blocks isn't just warmup/cooldown—it's proof. You finish each session demonstrating you had more capacity than you believed.
